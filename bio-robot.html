Bio-Inspired Subterranean Robot

Designed and developed a worm-inspired subterranean robot to measure underground CO₂ concentration in agricultural pipe networks.

Mechanical Design Sensors & Locomotion CPG-Based Control 3D Reconstruction

1. Overview

Designed and developed a worm-inspired subterranean robot to measure underground CO₂ concentration in agricultural pipe networks, enabling environmental analysis to study its potential effect on crop yield.

The system integrates articulated locomotion, live environmental sensing, and vision-based 3D reconstruction for subsurface mapping.

2. Problem Statement

Underground agricultural pipelines are narrow, grooved, and include sharp bends. Traditional inspection systems cannot navigate these constrained geometries while collecting environmental data.

The objective was to:

  • Traverse narrow underground pipes
  • Handle bends up to 90°
  • Collect real-time CO₂ data
  • Reconstruct internal pipe geometry

3. Mechanical Design

The robot was designed as a dual-module worm-inspired system with an articulated universal joint at the center. I engineered a custom geared articulated joint, a bending mechanism capable of 90° turns, and wheg-based traction extrusions to grip grooved pipe interiors.

The geared joint allowed adaptive bending while maintaining structural stability inside curved pipe sections.

4. Locomotion Strategy

To achieve coordinated motion inside confined geometry, I implemented a Central Pattern Generator (CPG)-inspired synchronization between the front and rear modules. This enabled phase-shifted rhythmic motion, stable propulsion in constrained environments, and continuous traction during bending.

5. Sensing & Mapping

The system integrates an SCD30 CO₂ sensor for environmental monitoring, a Raspberry Pi (compact form factor) for onboard processing, live data transmission, and camera-based 3D reconstruction for internal pipe mapping. The 3D reconstruction pipeline estimated pipe curvature and bend angle to assist navigation decisions.

6. Final Assembly

The final integrated platform elegantly combined the custom articulated gear joints, dual sensory suites, and Raspberry Pi onboard processing into a robust, functional form factor.

The completed dual-module assembly was highly compliant, able to traverse incredibly constrained spaces while structurally protecting sensitive internal electronics.

This unique architectural approach minimized the robot's physical profile, ensuring steady traversal within highly restrictive underground networks, turning harsh 90° pipeline joints from structural barriers into manageable terrain.

7. Experimental Validation

The robot was physically tested in grooved underground pipe structures. The field tests successfully validated mechanical adaptability in 90° bends, enabling the platform to traverse more than 20 meters and collect stable real-time CO₂ data throughout the operation.
